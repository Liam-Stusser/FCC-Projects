Steve Irwin

Steve was born on February 22nd in 1962

Steve learned to handle crocodiles at his parents' zoo and helped relocate over 100 crocs before taking over the zoo and renaming it Australia Zoo.

Steve met his wife Terri at his parents wildlife park, his father saying "it was love at first sight"

The Crocodile hunter became a near global success and had close to 500 million viewers

Steve was passionate about conservation and founded the Steve Irwin Conservation Foundation to help endangered species, the foundation was later renamed Wildlife Warriors Worldwide.

Steve passed away on September 4th in 2006 after a stingray pierced his chest with its barb during filming in the Great Barrier Reaf

In honor of Steve Irwin

Steve grew up in Queensland helping his parents at the zoo they opened there, learning to handle all sorts of animals

Steve learned to wrestle his first crocodile at age 9.

Steve and Terri spent their honeymoon trapping crocodiles together, the film would later become the first episode of Crocodile Hunter.

Steve went on to make a few more shows after Crocodile Hunter ended including: Croc Files, New Breed Vets, and Croc Week

Steve bought land in attempts to preserve the natural wildlife and considered conservation to be the most important part of his work

His funeral was held on September 9th in 2006, a public memorial was held and filmed at Australia Zoo with an estimated 300 million viewers.
